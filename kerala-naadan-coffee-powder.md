## Traditional Kerala Coffee Culture

### South Indian Filter Coffee

South Indian filter coffee is a ritual in Kerala households. A traditional Indian steel filter (*davra-tumbler* or *dabara set*) is used to brew a concentrated decoction that is then mixed with hot milk and sugar. Our Kerala Naadan Coffee Powder is specifically ground for this method — not too coarse, not too fine.

### How to Brew the Perfect Kerala Filter Coffee

1. Add 2–3 heaped tablespoons of coffee powder to the upper filter compartment
2. Press gently with the presser disc
3. Pour freshly boiled water (not boiling — around 93°C) over the powder
4. Wait 10–15 minutes for the decoction to drip through
5. Mix 30–40ml decoction with 150ml hot milk
6. Add sugar to taste
7. Pour between two cups (dabara-tumbler style) to create froth

> 💡 **Expert Tip:** For the best experience, always buy freshly roasted coffee and brew within 7–10 days of the roast date. Store in an airtight container away from sunlight.

### Naadan Coffee Tradition

"Naadan" simply means *local* or *traditional* in Malayalam. It carries a cultural weight — this is not commercial, not branded, not mass-produced. It is the coffee your grandmother made, now with full farm traceability.

Arabica beans (grown at higher altitude, like Chundale, Wayanad) produce a mild, aromatic, slightly acidic cup. Robusta beans are bolder, more bitter, and higher in caffeine. Many single-estate Kerala blends use a combination for balance.
