# Research Engine (Step 2) — User Guide

## Overview

The **Research Engine** discovers keywords using a **3-source priority ranking system**. It intelligently combines your knowledge with market data and AI insights to find the best keywords for your business.

### The Three Sources (Priority Order)

1. **Your Supporting Keywords** (Highest Priority — Score: +10)
   - Keywords you explicitly provided in Step 1
   - You know your business best
   - Always scored highest

2. **Google Autosuggest** (Medium Priority — Score: +5)
   - What real people actually search for
   - Provides market validation
   - Comes from actual Google search behavior

3. **AI Classification** (Quality Filter — Score: 0-10)
   - Analyzes if keywords match your stated business intent
   - Filters by type: transactional vs. informational vs. comparison
   - Adds semantic understanding of relevance

**Final Score** = Source Score + AI Score

## Quick Start

### Step 1: Provide Business Context

1. Enter your **pillar keywords** (2-5 main topics you want to rank for)
   - Example: "clove", "cardamom", "cinnamon"

2. For each pillar, add **supporting keywords** you're interested in
   - Example for "clove": "pure clove powder", "organic clove", "ground clove"
   - These are keywords you know your customers search for

3. **Select your business intent:**
   - **Ecommerce** — Selling physical products or services
   - **Blog/Content** — Writing educational content, guides, how-tos
   - **B2B/Supplier** — Wholesale, services, B2B sales
   - **Mixed** — Not sure yet, let AI help guide you

4. Click **"Run Research"**

### Step 2: Research Runs Automatically

The system will:
- Collect all your supporting keywords (Source 1)
- Fetch Google Autosuggest for each pillar (Source 2)
- Remove duplicate keywords
- Call AI to score and classify each keyword (Source 3)
- Return 20-50 ranked keywords in ~3-5 seconds per pillar

### Step 3: Review and Filter Results

You'll see keywords color-coded by source:
- **Green (User)** — Your input (highest confidence)
- **Blue (Google)** — Market-validated suggestions
- **Orange (AI)** — AI-generated additions

Filter by intent type to see only relevant results:
- **E-commerce?** Focus on "Transactional" keywords
- **Blog?** Focus on "Informational" keywords
- **B2B?** Focus on "Commercial" keywords

## Understanding the Scoring

### Score Tiers

| Score | Source | AI Score | What It Means |
|-------|--------|----------|---------------|
| 20 | User (+10) | +10 | Perfect: Your keyword + AI confirms it matches your intent |
| 15-19 | User (+10) | 5-9 | Excellent: Your keyword with strong AI support |
| 10-14 | Google (+5) | 5-9 | Very Good: Market-validated suggestion matching your intent |
| 5-9 | Google (+5) | 0-4 | Good: Google suggestion but weaker intent match |
| 0-4 | AI (0) | 0-4 | Consider: Mostly AI-generated, may not be relevant |

### Why Does Ranking Matter?

Higher-ranked keywords are "better" because they:
- **Higher User Score** → You confirmed it's relevant to your business
- **Higher Google Score** → More people actually search for it
- **Higher AI Score** → Better matches your stated business intent
- **Higher Confidence** → AI was more certain about the classification

## Understanding Intent Types

The AI classifies every keyword by **search intent** — what the searcher is actually trying to do:

### Transactional Intent
**The searcher is ready to buy.**
- Keywords: "buy clove online", "order clove powder", "clove shop"
- Best for: E-commerce businesses
- Typically easier to convert, higher intent-to-purchase
- Often have lower search volume but higher commercial value

### Informational Intent
**The searcher wants to learn.**
- Keywords: "how to use clove", "clove benefits", "clove history"
- Best for: Blog/content strategy
- Builds trust and authority
- Often have high search volume but lower immediate conversion

### Comparison Intent
**The searcher is comparing options.**
- Keywords: "clove vs pepper", "best clove brands", "clove alternatives"
- Best for: Competitive positioning
- Good for capturing undecided searchers
- Often on the path to purchase

### Commercial Intent
**B2B / wholesale-focused.**
- Keywords: "clove suppliers", "wholesale clove", "bulk clove powder"
- Best for: B2B / supplier businesses
- Usually lower volume but higher deal value
- Very specific buyer intent

### Local Intent
**The searcher wants location-specific results.**
- Keywords: "clove suppliers in Kerala", "where to buy clove near me"
- Best for: Local businesses or regional retailers
- Highly targeted, lower volume
- Strong for local SEO

## FAQ

### Q: What if I don't see my industry's keywords?

**A:** Make sure you're being specific in Step 1:
1. Use industry-specific pillar keywords (not generic terms)
2. Add 3-5 supporting keywords per pillar that you know are relevant
3. The more specific your input, the better the results
4. Example: Don't use "spice", use "clove", "cardamom", "cinnamon"

### Q: Why are some keywords marked "informational" when I want transactional?

**A:** The AI is categorizing the **search intent**, not judging the keyword:
- If you're e-commerce, you should focus on "transactional" keywords
- "Informational" keywords are great for blog content, but won't convert buyers
- Filter by intent to focus on what matters for your business goal

### Q: How long does research take?

**A:** Typically:
- **Per pillar:** 3-5 seconds
- **5 pillars:** 15-25 seconds total
- Results appear instantly in your dashboard

### Q: What if research returns 0 keywords?

**This shouldn't happen!** The system is designed to always return at least 20 keywords.

If it does:
1. Check your internet connection (Google Autosuggest needs it)
2. Make sure you added supporting keywords in Step 1
3. Try refreshing the page
4. Contact support if the problem persists

### Q: Can I edit the scores?

**A:** Not directly. The scores are calculated by the algorithm to ensure consistency.

However:
- **You can filter** by intent type to focus on what matters
- **You can reorder** by score to see your priorities
- **You can bookmark** keywords you want to track
- Scores update if you change your business intent in Step 1

### Q: Which keywords should I actually target?

**Quick rule of thumb:**

1. Start with **User keywords** (your green ones) — highest confidence
2. Add high-scoring **Google suggestions** (blue, score 10+)
3. Consider **AI suggestions** for exploration, but verify them

For e-commerce:
- Focus on transactional intent (score 15+)
- Ignore informational unless you also do blog content

For blog/content:
- Focus on informational intent (score 10+)
- Use comparison keywords for competitive posts
- Skip pure transactional keywords

For B2B/supplier:
- Focus on commercial intent (score 12+)
- Include local keywords if you have regional presence
- Skip consumer-focused keywords

### Q: How often should I re-run research?

**A:** Run research again if:
- Your business model changes
- You enter a new market or vertical
- Your target audience changes significantly
- You want to explore a new pillar keyword

Otherwise, your initial research is solid for 6-12 months.

### Q: How do I know if the scores are accurate?

**A:** Check the **reasoning** field for each keyword. It explains why that score was assigned.

Example reasonings:
- "Perfect e-commerce match" (high confidence)
- "Strong informational content keyword" (high confidence)
- "Low search volume but highly relevant" (medium confidence)

### Q: Can research fail?

**A:** The system is designed to never completely fail:

If Ollama (AI) is unavailable:
- You'll still get user keywords (+10) and Google suggestions (+5)
- AI scoring will be skipped, but you'll have results

If Google Autosuggest fails:
- You'll get user keywords and AI suggestions
- You can manually add more keywords if needed

If everything fails:
- Contact support — this is very rare

### Q: How is confidence calculated?

**Confidence** (0-100%) measures how certain the AI is about the classification:

- **95-100%:** Absolutely certain (e.g., "buy clove" is transactional)
- **85-94%:** Very confident (slightly ambiguous keywords)
- **70-84%:** Moderately confident (could be multiple intents)
- **Below 70%:** Low confidence (check the reasoning)

Filter out low-confidence results if you want only "safe" keywords.

### Q: How do I export my keywords?

**A:** In Step 3, you can:
- Download as CSV
- Copy to clipboard
- Share with team members
- Save as a keyword list for later

### Q: What's the difference between "volume" and "difficulty"?

**Volume** = How many people search for this keyword per month
- "high" = 1,000+ searches/month
- "medium" = 100-1,000 searches/month
- "low" = Under 100 searches/month
- "very_low" = Niche, under 50/month

**Difficulty** = How hard is it to rank on Google for this keyword
- "easy" = You can rank quickly (new domain OK)
- "medium" = You need decent domain authority
- "hard" = You need strong backlinks and authority

Strategy:
- **Starting out?** Target easy keywords with medium volume
- **Growing?** Mix easy and medium difficulty
- **Competing?** You can tackle hard difficulty, but needs time
